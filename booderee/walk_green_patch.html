                                    Walk Around Green Patch

Telegraph Creek Nature Trail
This circular walk is easy with signs describing interesting aspects of the vegetation along the way. It passes through tall eucalypt forest where the older trees provide hollows for birds and possums. Crossing serene, fern-lined creeks and swamps you may see freshwater crayfish foraging. The trail continues through woodland and heath. In spring delightful wildflower displays attract a wide variety of birds. Seats are provided along the way. The trail begins at the northern end of the Green Patch car park or where it is signposted along Jervis Bay Road near the Green Patch turn-off.

Telegraph Creek Nature Trail   2.4km   1hr

Green Patch to Bristol Point
Best attempted when the tide is low, this walk combines an easy, scenic ramble around the rocky shore with a short return through mature blackbutt forest. The rock platforms contain numerous plants and animals that have special adaptations so survive in their salty, weather-beaten environment. These plants and animals, like all the others in Booderee National Park, are protected and should not be disturbed. The blackbutt forest is great after dark when nocturnal animals can be seen. These animals are shy -- so be quiet and don't get too close. Access is from the eastern side of the Green Patch picnic area via a small bridge just before the beach. The rock platform can also be reached from the eastern end of the beach.

Green Patch Beach to Bristol Point   400m   10min
Green Patch via rock platform   500m   30min
