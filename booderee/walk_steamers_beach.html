                                    Walks Around Steamers Beach

Steamers Beach
Popular with fishers, Steamers Beach is a magnificent, isolated beach flanked by high cliffs. It is backed by steep sand dunes that are an unusual colour for Booderee and are thought to have been dumped there by a tsunami (a giant tidal wave). From Steamers Beach car park the trail takes you through tall eucalypt forest and tea-tree towards the coast. The last section includes a steep flight of stairs.

Steamers Beach car park to Steamers Beach   2.3km   1hr

Steamers Beach Trail
For an extended walk, head southwest from Steamers' Beach through the coastal scrub and woodland to Brooks Lookout. Superb views of Steamers Head and south to Ulladulla can be enjoyed from here. The circuit continues to St Georges Head then runs parallel to the Wreck Bay coastline. Short paths lead to secluded beaches. Whiting Beach is particularly beautiful and the last beach before turning right and walking around Blacks Waterhole and back to the car park. This circuit is an excellent choice for a full day's adventure, however it is 14.5km.

Steamers Beach car park to Steamers Beach   2.3km   1hr
Steamers Beach to Brooks Lookout   900m   30min
Brooks Lookout to St Georges Head   4km   2hr
St Georges Head to Blacks Waterhole   4.1km   2hr
Blacks Waterhole to Steamers Beach car park   3.2km   1.5hr
