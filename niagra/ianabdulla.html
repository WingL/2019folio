Ian Abdulla

Born 1947 Swan Reach. S.A

Ian Abdulla began painting in 1989 and was awarded South Australian Aboriginal artist of the year in 1991.
His vibrant paintings contain stories from his childhood spent around the Murray River region in South Australia.
Fishing, ploughing the fields, cycling and stealing lemons are just some of the topics that Abdulla describes with words and pictures.Ian Abdulla is also known for his popular picture books :-As I grew older and Tucker.
His work featured in the prestigious Adelaide Biennial exhibition 2000- " Beyond the Pale"
Niagara Galleries are also pleased to announce that as part of the Centenary of Federation celebrations, the Federation Festival is presenting two exhibitions by Ian Abdulla. River Themes, a series of paintings depicting the life in the Murray River region, at the RMIT Gallery from 8 May to 2 June and Picking Tomatoes, an installation at the Melbourne Museum from 9 to 27 May 2001

AWARDS:
1996 Best Painting section - National Aboriginal & Torres Strait Art Award
1996 Runner-up Australian Heritage Commission Art Award.
1994 Community Achievement Award, Barmera Council
1991 South Australian Aboriginal Artist of the Year
