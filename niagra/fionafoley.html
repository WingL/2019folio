Fiona Foley

Born 1964
Lives and works in Hervey Bay, Queenslan

"It's about putting indigenous people up front in the world, in every way - in fashion, in exhibitions, and in the gallery system"

For her first solo exhibition at Niagara Galleries, Fiona Foley will exhibit paintings and a recent series of photographs, Wild times called. Foley has photographed herself among Native Americans, in the North American landscape, challenging the way we typecast indigenous peoples in a romantic, nostalgic way. Her work is a powerful statement of defiance. Foley insists the viewer re-examines historical stereotypes.
Inspired by the natural environment of her hometown, Hervey Bay in south-east Queensland, Foley's paintings depict cockatoo feathers, dugong bones, mangrove seed pods and yam leaves.
Fiona Foley is an exceptionally talented artist. Over the past 15 years, she has forged a truly international career, exhibiting in Chile, Bolivia, Japan, Rusia, USA, Germany, South Africa and throughout Australia
